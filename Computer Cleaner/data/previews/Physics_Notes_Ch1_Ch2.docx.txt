Physics Notes – Chapter 1
Units & Measurement
• SI Units: meter (m), kilogram (kg), second (s)
• Prefixes: milli (10⁻³), centi (10⁻²), kilo (10³)
• Scalar vs. Vector: Scalars have only magnitude; Vectors have magnitude + direction
Motion Basics
• Distance (d): total length traveled (scalar)
• Displacement (Δx): straight-line change in position (vector)
• Speed: distance ÷ time (scalar)
• Velocity (v): displacement ÷ time (vector)
Acceleration Intro
• Acceleration (a): rate of change of velocity
• Formula: a = Δv / Δt = (vf – vi) / t
• Units: m/s²
Physics Notes – Chapter 2
Acceleration Vocabulary
• Acceleration: change in velocity per unit time
• Positive acceleration: speeding up in positive direction
• Negative acceleration (deceleration): slowing down or speeding up in negative direction
• Uniform acceleration: constant acceleration
Key Formulas
• Acceleration: a = Δv / Δt = (vf – vi) / t
• Final velocity: vf = vi + a·t
• Displacement: d = vi·t + ½·a·t²
• Displacement (alternate): d = (vi + vf)/2 · t
• Squared relation: vf² = vi² + 2·a·d
General Rules
• Positive velocity/acceleration = motion to the right/up
• Negative velocity/acceleration = motion to the left/down
• If velocity and acceleration have the same sign → object speeds up
• If velocity and acceleration have opposite signs → object slows down
