Journal Entries for “If You Can Keep it”
Read the assigned chapter/pages with pen, pencil, and/or highlighter in hand underlining words/phrases that hold significance for you.
Annotate the assigned text, using the stances and signposts as guides.  (Your margins should be filled with questions, comments, unknown vocabulary, references that you looked up, etc.)
After you've read and annotated, I would like a journal entry in your notebook containing a brief answer to the three stances. (You will underline more than you write--just the highlights.)
What surprised you?
What did the author think you already knew?
What challenged, changed, or confirmed what I knew? 
I'd also like you to include a vocabulary section of at least 5 words you looked up with a working definition.  You can also include any historical references/people/places/events you looked up in this section.
Include a quote from the text that most stood out to you and explain why you chose it.  (You can choose it for the meaning, or for the interesting/beautiful wording, or for the fact that you strongly disagree with it.)  
Write one question/topic for group discussion.
(For each chapter, your journal entry should be a 1-2 pages. Take a picture of your journal and post it to Google classroom.)
