Definitions:
Circle—set of all points in a plane equidistant from a given point called the center
Radius—segment whose endpoints are the center and any point on the circle
Chord—a segment whose endpoints are on a circle
Diameter—a special chord that contains the center of a circle
Secant—a line that intersects a circle in two points
Tangent—a line that intersects the circle in exactly one point, the point of tangency.  A ray and segment can also be tangent.

RADIUS AND DIAMETER The words radius and diameter are used for lengths as well as segments. For a given circle, think of a radius and a diameter as segments and the radius and the diameter as lengths.
