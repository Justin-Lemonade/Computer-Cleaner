SRA Physics – Semester 1 Part 1 Practice Test 
1D and 2D Kinematics (c2,c4), Forces (c3), Conservation of Energy (c6)
1. Ice Penguin Slide (1D Kinematics + Friction)
A 2.0 kg penguin wearing socks slides across an icy hallway at 6.0 m/s. The ice isn’t perfect, and the coefficient of kinetic friction between the penguin and the ice is 0.25.
a) What is the penguin’s acceleration?
b) How far does the penguin slide before coming to rest?
c) How long does it take for the penguin to stop?
2. Goat on a Mountain Path (Kinematics + Friction Energy)
A 4.0 kg mountain goat starts from rest at the top of a snowy 30° slope that is 5.0 m long. The goat’s hooves experience a coefficient of kinetic friction of 0.20.
a) Use kinematics to find the goat’s speed at the bottom.
b) How much energy was converted to heat through friction?
3. Launching a Wizard’s Spell (Vertical Motion)
A wizard launches a glowing orb straight upward with a speed of 12 m/s. The orb has a mass of 0.50 kg.
a) How high does the orb rise?
b) What is its speed when it returns to the launch point?
c) How long is the orb in the air?
4. Dragon Fireball (2D Projectile Motion)
A dragon launches a fireball from ground level at 20 m/s, aimed 35° above the horizontal.
a) How long is the fireball in the air?
b) What maximum height does it reach?
c) How far does it travel before hitting the ground?
5. Pirate Cannon Mishap (Horizontal Projectile)
A pirate accidentally fires a cannonball horizontally at 15 m/s from the top of a 45 m tall cliff.
a) How long does the cannonball take to hit the ocean?
b) How far from the base of the cliff does it land?
c) What is the speed of the cannonball just before impact?
6. Racing Rhino Brakes (Work–Energy + Friction)
A 1200 kg racing rhino charges forward at 22 m/s, then slams on the brakes. The coefficient of kinetic friction between the rhino’s shoes and the ground is 0.70.
a) How far does the rhino skid before stopping?
b) How long does it take to come to rest?
7. Blowdart and Wooden Tortoise (Momentum, Inelastic)
A 0.040 kg blowdart traveling at 400 m/s embeds itself in a 3.0 kg wooden tortoise resting on a frictionless surface.
a) What is the speed of the tortoise and dart immediately after the collision?
b) How much kinetic energy is lost during the collision?
8. Bumper-Car Hamsters (Momentum + Friction)
A 2.0 kg hamster zooms across a table at 5.0 m/s and collides head-on with a 1.0 kg hamster at rest. They stick together and slide across the surface, where the coefficient of kinetic friction is 0.30.
a) What is their speed right after the collision?
b) How far do they slide before stopping?
9. Superball Bounce of Doom (Energy + Momentum)
A 0.20 kg superball is dropped from a height of 3.0 m and bounces straight back up to a height of 1.8 m.
a) What is the ball’s speed just before it hits the ground?
b) What is its speed just after leaving the ground?
c) If the collision with the ground lasts 0.015 s, what is the average force the ground exerts on the ball?
10. Ninja Ramp Ambush (Energy → Momentum → Friction)
A 1.5 kg ninja slides silently down a frictionless ramp from a height of 2.0 m and collides elastically with a 0.5 kg training dummy at the bottom. After the collision, the dummy slides across a rough floor with a coefficient of kinetic friction of 0.40.
a) What is the ninja’s speed just before the collision?
b) What is the dummy’s speed immediately after the collision?
c) How far does the dummy slide before stopping?
11. Ice Hockey Puck Wizards (2D Collision)
A 1.5 kg enchanted puck glides east at 4.0 m/s and collides with a 2.0 kg cursed puck initially at rest on frictionless ice. After the collision, the enchanted puck moves at 2.5 m/s at an angle of 30° north of east.
a) Find the x- and y-components of the enchanted puck’s final momentum.
b) Determine the speed and direction of the cursed puck after the collision.
